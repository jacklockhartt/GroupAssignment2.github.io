Working In Information Technology

As required within this assignment an interview of an IT professional was conducted by Prototype3 team member Jack Lockhart who would go onto interview Patrick Shortt

What kind of work is done?

During the interview Jack asked the following question: Please tell us about your IT work. What exactly do you do?

Patrick responded describing that he worked as a "Technical Architect for Microsoft", going on to specify his role as being "A Solution Architect working with enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) software for Microsoft".

Jack followed up asking two more questions: Please tell us abou tthe industry you work in, and, what other kinds of work do you have to do? Patrick responded saying his industry is classified as "software" and that other kinds of work he is required to do involves "presentations at events, internal meetings, and lots of problem solving"

What kind of interactions happen?

Next up, Jack enquired as to Who are all the different people you interact with in your work? To which Patrick desrcibed that he works with "Microsoft customers and other businesses who work with Microsoft, called Microsoft partners", also mentioning that he works alot with "the Microsoft staff, locally and globally"

Following up the mention of clients, Patrick was then asked What about your interactions with clients or investors? To which patrick describes that "clients can range from medium size to large Enterprise customers and i have dealt with a lot of them." Patrick then listed a few examples of such clients; "Banks, Governments, Construction Companies,, "Retail Engineering, Manufacturing"

Elaborating on interactions, Patrick was asked to Tell us about your interactions with other IT professionals. Saying that he works with "many different types of IT professionals, support engineers, consultant, developers, solution architects, IT project managers, CIO's, CFO's, COO's, CEO's" (Chief Information Officer, Chief Financial Officer, Chief Operations Officer, Chief Executive Officer)

How is time spent?

To further understand the nature of Patricks job, Jack enquired as to What aspects of your work do you spend most time on? with Patrick responding that it is "fixing problems and [providing] support and guidance to our microsoft customerees and partners who are implmenting on of our ERP solutions. Additioanlly, Patrick responded to the enquiry of Can you share an exampl of the work you do that best captures the essence of the IT industry, to which Patrick replied "Google 'what is digital transformation?' that's what I do". A breif description ofdigital transformation is a methodology of problem solving by using digital technologies to solve problems that had no digital solutions.

What are the challenges of the work?

Finally, Jack asked What aspects of your work do you find most challenging? to which Patrick interestingly answered that it is "trying to keep up with the changing technology and the amount of work you have to do working for a large corporation".

As most clearly demonstrated from this last question, the interview of this IT professional has shown very intersting insights into what its like to be a working professional in the IT industry.
