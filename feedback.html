What went well?

Harry Oliver - One problem with the project that I joined late and we were late into starting to work together and getting communication up and running, however, what went well with this project was that once communication was established and everyone had access to the shared google doc we quickly began completing work. I wrote outlines of each segment of the assignment that needed completed with briefs of what needs to be contained and each member was extremely quick to fill out the blanks and completely satisfied with the amount of writing I thought was appropriate. While we struggled to get rolling the work ethic of each group member quickly alleviate this issue.

Conelio - We jumped on board after a few wobblies and we hit it hard and everyone understood the job at hand that needed to be done and it was done. The setting up was done quickly and efficiently and and thanks to Harry who put the framework together and it was easier to fill in the gaps. There was peace in the group and respect and it was actually pleasant to work with this mob even though we did not see each other physically.

Jack Lockhart - Overall I think the group managed rather well although we were all off to a late start which is understandable with people having different time frames for things they do in day to day life although when it came together it did come together, I have felt getting an interview straight away was very well timed on my behalf but also the fact that the group all added their own part with either large writings or text boxes for each other’s large writings.

Conelio - The university should understand that there is a huge diversity in people‘s lives in the course and trying to put something together and deal with the practicalities of life can be tricky, hence should give less assignments if there is group work that needs to be done.

Jack Lockhart - The way the team could have worked together if situations were different to output a more thorough document even though no matter how well a document is put together you can still always go better if better is the goal.

One thing that was surprising?

Conelio - How quickly I learned from zero to top notch in adapting to using technology and communications in a formal way and how effective it is to use this technology to achieve something sweet like this.

Jack Lockhart - The way the people had very similar ideas but also at the same time so different to each other which made it such a fun thing to learn about everyone else's ideas. The way they correlated together was awesome although mine was different im sure certain groups also had similar ideas.

One thing you learned from the group?

Harry Oliver - I believe that this group in particular has worked best with each of us tackling different requirements of the assignment. Two examples being me decoding the assignment document and presenting what needed to be written down so that not every member had to consult the document, and Jack Lockhart completing the interview segment of the assignment completely independently which meant that no other group member had to worry about organising it.

Conelio - Sharing the workload made all things easier and achievable and less stressful. There was no sweaty and no heart palpitations at all and the nights were good to sleep without the mind racing as much.

Jack Lockhart - The different paths people are willing to take in careers as well as the skills they need to accomplish these goals but also the way other people use their skills and ideas to create different goals or project ideas.
