Project Idea

The project idea that the group voted on was Harry Oliver’s idea which is extremely similar to the idea presented by Roberto, the fact that two team members had coincidentally developed very similar ideas is very beneficial to the vision of the project as it allows the two members to contribute their own ideas for their projects towards one group project. Additionally the two other members provide an outsider perspective of the idea as it develops and can provide an objective opinion on the design choices we make. A brief of the project idea is as follows:The project idea was a mobile application which would organise and present political parties policies during an election cycle. The purpose of the application would be to clearly present each individual policy idea put forward by major political parties on the key issues, following this presentation would be an expert analysis to verify the validity of the policy suggestions as well as to present what the effects of such a policy would be. The only alternative to this concept that I know of is the Australian Broadcasting Corporation’s collaboration with RMIT University in its program called ‘Fact Check’ but this segment is nowhere near as fleshed out and developed as it should be.

This project was developed from the understanding of the growing use of misinformation in election cycles and my desire to preserve Australian democracy to prevent it from corrupting like so many others. Roberto’s contribution differed in that it would have a social media function within the app where users could present to other users how the policy would affect them, this would allow for voters to be able to sympathise with those who would be negatively/positively affected by policy, i.e. if a bill sought to reduce the weekly pension amount its easier for younger voters to be unsympathetic towards the elderly but seeing a real human express how much more difficult the loss of income would be allows voters to make decisions that benefit others as well as themselves.
